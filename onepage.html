Project Description

Clean drive RPDA is a road side emission monitoring system to detect and log vehicle exhaust pollutants in real time. The system uses gas sensors, vehicle detection sensor, a servo motor adjustable mechanism and camera module, to measure pollutants such as CO2, NOX, HC, and CO from passing vehicles. Data is recorded lacally and transmitted to a server for analysis. Enabling the identification of high emission vehicles and supporting environmental research or traffic pollution studies.

Key objectives

  • Objective 1 — Pollutants measurement.
  • Objective 2 — High emmission vehicles identification.
  • Objective 3 — Real time operation.

Scope & constraints:

It includes sensors such as MQ-7, MQ-135 to measure harmful gases and particulates from passing vehicles. A piezoelectric sensor is used for vehicle detection, while an ESP32-CAM captures license plate images for identification. The ESP32-CAM also serves as the main controller, processing data, storing it on an SD card, and sending it to the cloud through Wi-Fi.
